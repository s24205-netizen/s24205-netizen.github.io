🎯 Goal of the Project

We observed that butterflies often became trapped within the original footbridge, finding it difficult to escape. Our new design specifically addresses this issue, ensuring a safer passage for local insect populations.

✨ UV-Reflective Coating

To prevent birds and insects from colliding with the glass, we decided to apply a UV-reflective coating. This makes the panels visible to wildlife while maintaining transparency for humans.

🔍 How Does the Coating Work?

Designed for use on various materials—including glass, metals, and fabrics—UV-reflective coatings interact with light spectrums beyond human perception. However, because birds and butterflies possess UV-sensitive eyes, these coatings remain visible to them as a solid barrier!

📏 Information & Dimensions

With a length of 12.9 meters, a width of 1.8 meters, and a peak height of 3.5 meters, the bridge closely mirrors the scale of the original footbridge, making it perfectly functional for daily use.

🦋 Characteristics of Butterflies

Butterflies exhibit phototaxis, an instinctive drive to move toward light sources. This biological behavior leads them to navigate toward the sun or artificial bulbs, as they use bright points to orient themselves during flight.

🌡️ The Greenhouse Effect

Will the sunlight shine into the bridge and make the inside too hot? The answer is no! The UV-reflective coating doesn't just protect wildlife; it also significantly reduces the greenhouse effect. By deflecting excess solar radiation, it ensures the interior remains at a comfortable temperature for everyone crossing it.

🔮 Things We Will Consider in the Future

We acknowledge that light reflected from the glass side panels might impact the surrounding community. However, we were unable to execute a formal experiment to quantify these reflections during this initial phase.
