01. The Objective

Goal of the Project

We observed that butterflies often became trapped within the original footbridge, finding it difficult to escape. Our new design specifically addresses this issue, ensuring a safer passage for local insect populations.

02. Material Science

UV-Reflective Coating

To prevent birds and insects from colliding with the glass, we decided to apply a UV-reflective coating. This makes the panels visible to wildlife while maintaining visual transparency for humans.

03. Technology

How Does the Coating Work?

Designed for use on various materials—including glass, metals, and fabrics—UV-reflective coatings interact with light spectrums beyond human perception. However, because birds and butterflies possess UV-sensitive eyes, these coatings remain visible to them.

04. Specifications

Information

With a length of 12.9 meters, a width of 1.8 meters, and a peak height of 3.5 meters, the bridge closely mirrors the scale of the original footbridge, ensuring optimal utility and seamless integration into the environment.

05. Biology

Characteristics of Butterflies

Butterflies exhibit phototaxis, an instinctive drive to move toward light sources. This biological behavior leads them to navigate toward the sun or artificial bulbs, as they use bright points to orient themselves during flight.

06. Thermodynamics

The Greenhouse Effect

Will the sunlight shine into the bridge and make the inside too hot? The answer is no. The UV-reflective coating also significantly reduces the greenhouse effect, ensuring the interior remains at a comfortable temperature.

07. Next Steps

Considerations for the Future

We acknowledge that light reflected from the glass side panels might impact the surrounding community. However, we were unable to execute a formal experiment to quantify these reflections during the initial phase.
