Wah Yan Eco-Bridge

An architectural project designed with heart to protect our beloved butterflies and local wildlife through sustainable design and care.

Overview

Harmonizing Buildings and Nature

Welcome to the official platform for the Wah Yan Eco-Bridge project. Discover how we are redesigning our campus bridge to create a safe, welcoming connection for students while deeply respecting the delicate ecosystem of our school grounds, especially our beautiful butterfly populations.

01. Our Purpose

Answering the Call of Laudato Si'

In Form 3, the IDEEA subject uniquely challenges us to combine all the skills we have learned over the previous two years to create a meaningful, real-world product.

With this year's theme dedicated to the environment, our foundation is deeply inspired by our Catholic faith and Pope Francis's 2015 encyclical, Laudato Si'. We believe that God created this magnificent world and entrusted us to be its stewards. Answering the heartfelt call for the care of our common home, we firmly believe the environment is not a resource to be exploited, but a precious gift that we must actively protect for generations to come.

02. The Challenge

Protecting Our Wildlife

Looking closely at our own campus, we noticed a heartbreaking ecological issue. Currently, our bridge leading to Gordon Wu Hall is an open structure with no windows, flanked by beautiful forests on both sides. For butterflies traveling between these green spaces, the easiest and most direct flight path is straight through the bridge.

However, the existing bridge features a semi-transparent domed roof. As sunlight beams down through this material, it tricks the butterflies. Drawn to the light above, they fly upward instead of straight through. Sadly, they become trapped inside the dome and perish. Our goal is to prevent them from entering the bridge interior in the first place.

03. Phototaxis

Drawn to the Light

Butterflies naturally fly toward light, a biological instinct known as phototaxis. This instinct helps them use the sun to navigate and find their way around the forests. However, this is the exact reason the old semi-transparent roof confused them and caused them to fly upward into the dome instead of flying safely across.

04. Design

The Five Key Features

  • 1. UV-Reflective Coating

    We are installing tempered glass panels with a UV-reflective coating to fully enclose the open bridge. This creates a solid barrier that wildlife can visually detect, successfully preventing collisions.

  • 2. Lightweight GORE-TEX Roofing

    We are replacing the semi-transparent dome with lightweight GORE-TEX fabric roofing. This blocks the confusing top-down sunlight, while remaining highly breathable and remarkably durable against all weather conditions.

  • 3. Louvered Ventilation Panels

    Because sealing the bridge creates an enclosed space, we are adding louvered ventilation panels to ensure continuous fresh air circulation and climate control.

  • 4. Double-Link Plastic Chains

    Since we cannot install glass across the open walkways where people pass, we are hanging a double-link plastic chain curtain at the doorways. This allows humans to walk through easily but physically deters insects from flying inside.

  • 5. Maintenance Access Walkway

    We have integrated a maintenance access walkway with a perimeter safety railing. This dedicated exterior walkway allows for the safe and easy cleaning of the exterior glass windows.

05. UV Coating

A Clear Solution

By putting up glass windows to block the butterflies from entering the bridge, we created a new problem: would they simply crash into the clear glass instead? To prevent these collisions, our windows use an innovative ultraviolet coating.

This special coating reflects ultraviolet light. Humans cannot see this light, but the eyes of birds and butterflies are highly sensitive to it. When they see the glowing UV reflection on the glass, they perceive it as a solid wall and fly safely around the exterior of the bridge rather than crashing into it.

06. Temperature

Beating the Greenhouse Effect

Enclosing the previously open bridge with windows naturally raises another concern: will the trapped air act like a greenhouse and make the bridge unbearably hot? Fortunately, our design solves this issue entirely.

The specialized glass and its UV coating not only prevent wildlife collisions but also effectively block and reflect harsh solar heat. Combined with our new opaque GORE-TEX roof and built-in ventilation fans, the interior space remains cool, shaded, and incredibly comfortable.

07. Dimensions

Bridge Measurements

Built to perfectly fit our campus landscape, the renewed bridge's structural frame remains exactly 12.9 meters long, 1.8 meters wide, and 3.5 meters high. We kept the original dimensions to ensure the crossing is strong, secure, and familiar for everyone to use.

08. Future

Looking Ahead

As caring designers, we understand that introducing large glass walls into a forest space can slightly alter the surrounding microclimate. Moving forward, our next goal is to meticulously measure any temperature shifts near the bridge exterior to ensure the entire school environment, and its beautiful butterfly population, stays balanced and healthy.
